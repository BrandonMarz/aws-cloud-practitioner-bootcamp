Cloud Computing is the on-demand access and sharing of IT resources over the internet 
IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS are different in how they are managed and controlled
IaaS is on-demand and allows for the self management of apps (AWS EC2) (DIY)
PaaS is a ready to use platform for developers to build test and deploy applications directly (AWS Elastic Beanstalk)
SaaS is fully managed ready to use apps over the internet (Google Workspace)(DFY)
Cloud vs On-premise
Cloud - Rent storage and computing power from AWS
On Premise- You buy the servers, set them up in your office and pay for the electricity and IT team to manage them
CapEx vs OpEx
CapEx (Capital Expenditure) - Buying your own equipment upfront
OpEx (Operating Expenditure) - Renting or paying as you go (imagine using EC2 for the hours you run the server)
